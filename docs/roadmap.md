# Roadmap

Order reflects current intent, not promises. Feedback moves things:
[open an issue](../../issues).

## Now

- **Device polish** — hardening the iOS Shortcut and Android share-target
  flows on real hardware.

## Next

- **"I flag this too" goes live** — real flag counts backed by real storage;
  the first genuinely live crowd signal.
- **Live video metadata** — real titles, authors and thumbnails on cards
  (starting with YouTube).
- **Model-assisted intent reads** — an AI pass over title/thumbnail as a
  labeled *hint* alongside the crowd, never the verdict.
- **Browser extension** — the verdict as an overlay on desktop YouTube/X,
  no copy-paste.

## Later

- **Real community accounts** — reporters, reputations, notes with authorship.
- **Native apps** — iOS Share Extension and Android app, once the community
  layer earns the install.

## Non-goals

- A background process that watches your screen. Grain is toggle-and-ask,
  always.
- Pretending an algorithm can settle what the crowd is still arguing about.
